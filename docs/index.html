🔍 Analysis Techniques

  • 1
    P-Curve Analysis
    Detects clustering of p-values just below the 0.05 threshold (0.040–0.050 range). High clustering ratios (>50%) indicate potential p-hacking or selective reporting of marginally significant results.
  • 2
    Statistical Power Analysis
    Estimates post-hoc power based on reported effect sizes and sample sizes using Cohen's formulas. Flags studies with estimated power below 80%, indicating insufficient sample sizes to reliably detect the claimed effects.
  • 3
    Impossible Value Detection
    Identifies mathematically impossible statistics: p-values outside [0,1], correlations outside [-1,1], implausibly large effect sizes (d>5), and inverted confidence intervals where lower ≥ upper bounds.
  • 4
    Methods Completeness Scoring
    Evaluates 8 critical methodological elements: sample size justification, randomization, blinding, control conditions, inclusion/exclusion criteria, outcome pre-specification, analysis plans, and effect size reporting.
  • 5
    Reproducibility Checklist (TOP Guidelines)
    Checks 5 reproducibility criteria: data availability, code availability, pre-registration, materials availability, and replication attempts. Aligned with Transparency and Openness Promotion (TOP) guidelines.
  • 6
    Ethics and Transparency Audit
    Verifies presence of IRB/ethics approval, informed consent statements, conflict of interest disclosures, and funding acknowledgements — all required by major journal policies.
